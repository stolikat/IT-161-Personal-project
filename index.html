Peak Elevation State Volcanic Status Notable Facts
Mount Rainier14,411 ft (4,392 m)WAActive stratovolcanoMost glaciated peak in the lower 48; lahar risk to nearby valleys.
Mount Shasta14,179 ft (4,322 m)CAActive stratovolcanoMultiple overlapping cones; culturally significant to local tribes.
Mount Adams12,281 ft (3,743 m)WADormant / potentially activeBroad slopes and large lava flows; popular non-technical climb.
Mount Hood11,239 ft (3,429 m)ORActive stratovolcanoFumaroles near summit; year-round skiing at Timberline.
Glacier Peak10,541 ft (3,213 m)WAActive stratovolcanoRemote; highly explosive eruptions in the last 15,000 years.
Mount Baker10,786 ft (3,288 m)WAActive stratovolcanoNotorious snowfall; steaming at Sherman Crater.
Mount St. Helens8,366 ft (2,549 m)WAActive stratovolcano1980 lateral blast reshaped the mountain; ongoing monitoring.
